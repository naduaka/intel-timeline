Intel Milestones

A Legacy of Sustainable Innovation

1994

First Environmental Report

Intel publishes its first public environmental report, setting a precedent for tech industry transparency.

This landmark report detailed Intel's chemical usage, waste generation, and energy consumption — one of the earliest moves by a major semiconductor company to publicly disclose its environmental footprint. It laid the groundwork for decades of accountability to come.

Environmental Reporting
1998

Energy Conservation Goals

Intel sets aggressive internal targets to reduce energy use across its global manufacturing operations.

By implementing energy-efficient equipment and optimizing fab processes, Intel began a multi-year effort to decouple business growth from energy consumption growth. These early investments saved millions of kilowatt-hours annually and influenced how the entire chip industry approached efficiency.

Energy Efficiency
2008

Largest Renewable Energy Purchaser

Intel becomes the largest voluntary purchaser of green power in the US, according to the EPA.

Intel purchased over 1.3 billion kilowatt-hours of renewable energy certificates in 2008 alone, equivalent to powering all of its US operations with clean energy. This achievement earned recognition from the EPA Green Power Partnership and set a new bar for corporate renewable energy commitment in the tech sector.

Renewable Energy
2012

Water Restoration Program

Intel launches a comprehensive water restoration initiative targeting water-stressed regions near its facilities.

Chip fabrication is water-intensive, and Intel's water restoration program aimed to return as much water to local watersheds as its manufacturing consumed. By partnering with conservation nonprofits and investing in recycled water infrastructure, Intel restored billions of gallons of water across Arizona, Oregon, and beyond.

Water Conservation
2016

Conflict-Free Supply Chain

Intel becomes the first semiconductor company to achieve a verified conflict-free supply chain for its microprocessors.

After years of auditing and working with smelters, Intel eliminated minerals sourced from conflict zones in the Democratic Republic of Congo from its supply chain. This achievement set an industry standard and pushed other major tech companies to follow, reshaping how the semiconductor industry thinks about ethical sourcing.

Responsible Sourcing
2020

RISE Strategy Launch

Intel launches its RISE strategy — Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling — with 2030 goals across people and planet.

The RISE strategy set ambitious 2030 targets: achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions in global operations, conserving 60 billion gallons of water, diverting 90% of waste from landfills, and reaching 100% renewable electricity. It represented Intel's most comprehensive sustainability framework to date and tied executive compensation to sustainability metrics.

RISE Strategy
2030

Net-Zero & Net Positive Water

Intel's 2030 target: achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions and net positive water use across all global operations.

By 2030, Intel aims to restore more water than it consumes (net positive water), achieve net-zero Scope 1 and 2 greenhouse gas emissions, power 100% of global operations with renewable electricity, and send zero manufacturing waste to landfills. These goals represent the culmination of over three decades of environmental commitment.
